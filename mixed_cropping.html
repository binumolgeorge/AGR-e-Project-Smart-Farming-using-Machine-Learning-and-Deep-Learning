What is Mixed Cropping?

Mixed cropping is an agricultural practice where two or more crops are grown together on the same piece of land. This method aims to maximize the utilization of resources such as sunlight, water, and nutrients, leading to increased crop yields and reduced risk of crop failure.

By growing compatible crops together, farmers can improve soil fertility, control pests and diseases naturally, and promote biodiversity. Mixed cropping also helps in efficient land use and can provide a variety of produce, contributing to food security and economic stability.

Our smart farming tool assists farmers in identifying the best crop combinations based on scientific data and local farming conditions, ensuring sustainable and profitable agriculture practices.
